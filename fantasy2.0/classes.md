# Classes
Unless otherwise specified, adventurers begin as commoners at level 0 and level up from there by gaining XP. When an adventurer reaches level 1, they pick a class(or may roll a D20 for a random one on the table below). At level 1, they are granted certain benefits. At level 2 and higher, an adventurer gains benefits by rolling twice per level on the boons table for their class.

### Boons Table(2d6)

### At level 1
#### Skills
- Daggers
- Stealth
- Sleight of Hand
- Acrobatics
- Perception

#### Grit
You have 3 grit, which recovers on a rest. You may spend it on the following options.
- Dodge: Reduce incoming damage by D6.
- Sprint: Double your movement this turn without spending an action.
- Focus: Add an extra D6 bonus to a roll already benefiting from a skill proficiency.
- Hide: Attempt to hide without using an action.

### Class Abilities

#### Street Rat
You have a keen eye for the illicit, and you're able to easily find the signs criminal contacts leave for their colleagues. If the black market is active around you, you will find it after investigating the locals for an hour. This includes fences, dealers, smugglers, informants, assassins, mercenaries, corrupt guards, and gangs.

#### Rooftop Runner
You now have expertise in acrobatics and take half the normal damage from falling.

#### Cat Burglar
You now have expertise in stealth and may spend one grit to gain advantage on a stealth check.

#### Slippery
You have advantage on checks to resist being grappled or restrained. 

#### Improvised shield
You may freely use certain objects such as thick books, briefcases, and furniture as shields, providing 3 armor value. Durability varies by plausibility.

You gain an instrument from the list below. Every rest, you may practice your instrument to prepare two random spells from the arcane spell list until the next rest. You may consume 2 grit to play your instrument, casting a prepared spell.

- Viola

### Class Abilities

#### Variant spells
When you roll a random spell to prepare for the day, you may prepare a particular variant of the rolled spell. Casting higher level spells increases the cost by 3 grit per level(5 for 2nd, 8 for 3rd, etc).

#### Playing By Ear
When you see someone cast a spell, you may expend 2 grit to memorize it until your next rest, replacing another prepared spell. The cost increases by 2 grit per level for spells 2nd level or higher.

#### Improv Performance
Forego preparing one spell and instead prepare a random style from the table below. When using this style to perform an improvised performance, the player describes a spell based on the name and negotiates a cost in grit with the keeper in order to cast  it.
1. Acceso: Ignited, on fire
2. Bellicoso: Warlike, aggressive
3. Capriccioso: Capricious, unpredictable, volatile
4. Dolente: Sorrowful, plaintive
5. Eroico: Heroic
6. Fugue: Literally "flight"; hence a complex and highly regimented contrapuntal form in music

#### Have at you!
You gain expertise with swords.

#### Silver Tongue
You may use bardic magic by singing.

#### Vicious Mockery
You make a magically infused insult against a creature who can understand you. Target  must save or take 2d6 damage from the psychic strain.

#### Practice, practice, practice!
You may prepare one additional spell each rest.

#### Oath
Make an oath, which grants you magical powers such as smite and serves as your guiding ethic. Breaking your oath costs you your oath powers.
- Vengeance: I will avenge the fallen by slaying their murderers.
- Protection: I will defend the powerless without exception.
- Redemption: When able, I will offer wicked creatures a chance to redeem themself.
- Justice: I will right all wrongs I encounter, or die trying.
- Truth: I will neither lie, nor deceive, nor willingly allow others to do so.

### Class Abilities

#### Oath Power: Sword of Vengeance
You may declare a non-magical sword as your sword of vengeance, making it a +1 sword. You may spend one grit to raise this sword and declare your intention to avenge a fallen creature. Using this sword, attacks against its killers now have advantage. You may spend another grit to raise your sword into the air and feel it pull in the general direction of the nearest killer.

#### Oath Power: Shield of Protection
Any metal shield you wear gleams in the sunlight, providing +1 more armor value. When wielding such a shield, spells damage against you is halved. You may spend one grit to protect others standing adjacent to you with your shield, granting them its benefits(it still loses durability when blocking damage) until the start of your next turn.

#### Oath Power: Redeemer
You now have expertise with persuasion. You have advantage on persuasion checks when appealing to the target's better nature.

#### Oath Power: Sword of Justice
You may declare a non-magical sword as your sword of vengeance, making it a +1 sword. It deals an extra 2d6 damage against humanoids who are violent wrongdoers, including corrupt authorities.

#### Oath Power: Compell Truth
Spend one grit and aim your sword at a creature, demanding they answer a question truthfully. Until you point your sword away, they cannot knowingly tell a lie. You may spend another grit and make a contested magic check to compell them to speak if they refuse to.

#### Oath Power: Call Steed
Once per rest, you may spend one grit to call upon the heavens for a steed. A pale horse will appear in a bright flash of light. If you dismiss or are separated from the horse, it vanishes in a bright flash of light.

#### Oath Power: Righteous Healer
You have a pool of 10 hearts, which recovers on a rest. You may expend 1 or more and touch a creature to heal it that many hearts. If a creature has died in the last minute, you may expend one heart to allow it to retry its death saving throw.

You gain a spellbook. Choose four arcane schools of magic and add the first spell in each school's spell list to your spellbook. Each rest, you may meditate to charge your spellbook. #### Soul Stone
You carry a magical crystal which stores up to 3 spirit points that recover on a rest. You may expend a spirit point to cast a spell. You may hold the soul stone over a fresh humanoid corpse to recover 1 spirit point and turn the corpse to ashes. It is important to note that the undead you may raise cannot benefit from healing, except if they cast the Soul Drain spell.

#### Necromancy Spellcasting
As long as your soul stone is within 15 feet of you, you may use it to expend spirit points and cast spells. A cantrip has a 1 in 6 chance to consume 1 spirit point, and leveled spells consume one spell per level. At 1st level, you know either Beckon Spirits(Cantrip) or Minor Reanimation(Cantrip). 19. Soul Drain(3rd)

### Class Abilities

#### Learn Random Spell
Roll a D20 on the necromancer spell list, re-rolling if you roll a spell you already know.

#### Lichdom
Your body is now an undead puppet, your soul residing in your soul stone. If your soul stone is shattered, you die. When your body reaches 0 hearts and you fail a death save, it falls limp. Provided your soul stone is within 15 feet of your body, you may re-make your death save every turn to raise your body with 1 heart. You may reform a new body from a corpse. Your body does not benefit from healing, except from the soul drain spell. You do not need a body to cast spells or order the undead you have raised.

#### Corpse Battery
When you cast a spell, you may power it using one of the undead you have raised, turning the undead to dust. Undead raised by Minor Reanimation or Raise Horde may be used for a 1st level. Undead raised by Raise Undead can be used for a 2nd level spell. Undead raised using raise abomination may be used for a 3rd level spell.

# Spells and spellcasting

## Spell variants
The default version of a spell is considered the 1st level, as it consumes 1 resource to cast. In addition, there are other variants with altered effects and costs. When you cast a cantrip spell, you roll a D6 and only consume the resource if your roll is a 1. A 2nd level spell consumes 2 resources, a 3rd consumes 3, etc.

## Arcane Spell list

### 1 Fire Magic
1. Burning Hands (1st): 15ft cone. Creatures must save to halve 3d6 damage.
2. Fire bolt(C): Ranged attack that deals 2d6 damage on a hit.
3. Bonfire(C): Create fire in 5ft cube for an hour. D6 damage to those inside flame.
4. Shape Fire(C): Move, extinguish, grow, or sculpt a flame that fits in a 5ft cube.
5. Scorching Ray(2nd): Fire two bolts of fire, each dealing 3d6 damage on a hit.
6. Fireball(3rd): A bolt of fire explodes into a 30ft sphere of flames. Creatures inside must save to halve 4d6 damage.

### 2 Water Magic
1. Create water(1st): Produce 100 gallons of water in container, as spray, or as rain over an area.
2. Shape Water(C): Move, freeze, color, or sculpt up to a 5ft square of water. Lingering effects last 1 hour.
3. Cold Ray(C): Ranged attack that deals 4d6 damage. On hit, target saves or has limbs frozen to ground, immobilizing. Freezes up to a 10ft cube of water to ice.
4. Ice Knife(1st): Fire a shard of ice at one target. On a hit, it deals 3d6 damage to the target. On a miss, it explodes and all creatures within 10ft must save to halve 2d6 damage.
5. Ice Shards(2nd): Fire a stream of ice shards at a 10ft square area. All creatures inside must save to halve 4d6 damage.
6. Cone of Cold(3rd): Produce a 30ft cone of freezing air. Creatures inside must save to halve 4d6 damage. 

### 3 Earth Magic
1. Grease(1st): Create slick patch(30ft square) of grease for 1 hour. If ignited, it burns for 1 minute, dealing d6 damage per turn.
2. Shape Earth(C): You may move a 1 meter cube of soil up to 30 feet.
3. Acid Arrow(1st): Ranged attack. On a hit, target takes 3d6 damage, and does so at the end of their next two turns.
4. Tremor(1st): All stone surfaces within 60ft shatter violently, forcing creatures adjacent to them to save or take 3d6 damage.
5. Plant Growth(2nd): Select a 10ft cube, wherein plants and seeds sprout into mature plants. You may choose the plants to flower, or to produce mature fruit.
6. Shape Stone(3rd): Precisely carve stone that fits into a 10ft cube, creating up to five items in the process, or one structure.

### 4 Air Magic
1. Cloud(1st): Create clouds within a 20ft cube that obscure vision and last 1 hour.
2. Shocking Grasp(C): Make an unarmed attack, which deals 10 damage on a hit.
3. Gust(C): Create a strong gust of wind. You may focus it on one creature, forcing them to save to a void being knocked over.
4. Poison Spray(C): One target within 10ft saves to halve 4d6 damage.
5. Fly(2nd): For 1 hour, you fly 60 feet per turn.
6. Lightning Bolt(3rd): Ranged 4d6 damage. On hit, roll a D6, and on a 5-6 chain another target(unlimited).

### 5 Force Magic
1. Magic Missile(1st): Fire 3 spectral darts that always hit and deal 2 damage each.
2. Shield(1st): Until the start of your next turn, you have 6 armor value.
3. Mage Armor(1st): Create a magical barrier around yourself that has 2 armor value and 10 durability value.
4. Telekinesis(2nd): Lift an object up to 300ft. May use to grapple a creature, who may save to resist the spell.
5. Tiny Hut(3rd): Create a half-submerged sphere of force 10ft in diameter centered on the caster, which lasts for 8 hours. The magic barrier allows air to pass through and may be shattered by a single attack dealing 50 or more damage.
6. Force Cage(3rd): Create a 15ft hollow box of force around a point in sight. It has 300 hearts, and lasts until shattered.

### 6 Psychic Magic
1. Mind Spike(1st): Target saves or takes 2d6 damage and reveals location for 1 hour.
2. Scry(2nd): Holding a possession(hat, pen, strand of hair) belonging to your target, you can see the target's surroundings for 1 minute.
3. Telepathy(C): Silently send and receive messages for to one creature you can see. Lasts 1 minute.
4. Befriend(1st): Target saves for 24 hours immunity to this spell, or consider caster friend for 24 hours.
5. Tongues(1st): All creatures understand your speech for 1 hour.

### 7 Illusion Magic
1. Disguise Self(1st): Disguise yourself for 1 hour. Changes are ephemeral.

3. Light(C): You create a point of light on an object that lasts for 1 hour and has a 60ft range.
4. Magic Mouth(2nd): You create a magical mouth which speaks a phrase or sound according to a trigger you define. This lasts 24 hours. If you cast magic mouth on a book, it will instead read the contents of the book when triggered.
5. Major Illusion(2nd): Create an illusion which fits in a 20ft cube and lasts one hour. May move within the cube, produce light, and produce sound, but does not react to its surroundings.

3. Haste(2nd): Target saves or gains double movement and can perform 2 actions per turn for one minute.
4. Grow(3rd): For an hour, target grows five times larger. 
5. Shrink(3rd): For an hour, target shrinks five times smaller.
